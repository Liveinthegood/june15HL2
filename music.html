Hyaptia's first album was recorded in Nashville, Tennesee for SRO Records in 1986. As one side of the record was country music and the other featured funny songs such as "Telephone Man" and "Peter the Meter Reader," it was called The Two Sides of Hyapatia Lee. "Rub-a-Dub-Dub" from the comedy side was featured on the Dr. Demento Show and made it to number three in the Funny Five countdown. In addition to writing and performing music, Hyapatia also produces music videos.

Hyapatia suffers from a rare condition known as panhypopituitarism, a complete lack of pituitary gland functions. As the pituitary gland is the master gland, this condition is life-threatening for several reasons. There is no cure. Contributions towards Hyapatia's Go Fund Me are always greatly appreciated.
